About UWC Science Collective

Mission

We are current students from the United World College Changshu China, experienced and driven in the life sciences. We are inviting you to join us in setting up a four-week virtual research program specialising in Neurosciences, built to equip students in gaining research design, data collection, and research presentation skills. This will culminate in a virtual conference, whereby students will have the opportunity to share a Capstone Project with fellow peers and invited academics.

Additionally, we are building a research journal to support students, aiming at opening the opportunity for UWC students to publish their scientific research and build a culture of doing science at UWC.

Why did we create this?

Students at UWC CSC recently had the opportunity to take part in a 10-month course in the Neuroscience and Cell Biology, and Math and Economics, taught by Nobel Laureates Thomas Sudhof and Eric Maskin respectively. Over this period, the students have been able to explore college-level content in the select fields and have been working on term papers exploring the topics further. As students from the Neuroscience and Cell Biology program, we hope to share what we’ve learned, provide additional skills that have been helpful in our journey in the program, as well as research overall.

Having experienced the difficulties and joys that come with the process, we hope to create a platform in which more students in the UWC community can also engage in the field, and gain valuable skills along the way.
